Exploration B — Left Border + Earth Tones

A thick left border carries the type signal. Colors shift from traffic-light primaries to earth tones: terracotta for risk, olive for opportunity, sage for pattern. Warmer, quieter palette. The border is the only color — everything else is neutral. Type label sits inline with source for density.

Risk 3 of 5 participants
Users abandon the session setup flow when the objective field has no guidance or examples
Participants paused for 8–15 seconds on the objective field before either typing something generic or navigating away. Two participants explicitly said they didn't know what was expected. The empty state provides no scaffolding.
Opportunity Cross-session synthesis
Confidence level in the summary strip could link directly to the supporting evidence
PMs consistently asked "why this confidence level?" after reading the summary. A direct link from the confidence indicator to the underlying data points would eliminate this follow-up question entirely.
Pattern 4 of 5 participants
Participants consistently look for a "done" or "complete" action after reviewing results
After reviewing results, participants looked for a way to mark the session as complete. The current flow has no explicit completion action — sessions just become inactive, creating ambiguity.
Risk PM feedback — 2 incidents
The shared view exposes internal run IDs in the URL, confusing stakeholders
Two PMs separately asked what the alphanumeric string in the shared URL meant. Internal identifiers leaking into shared surfaces undermine the "readable labels" principle.
Opportunity Workflow observation
Design Ops module completion dates could auto-populate the "next steps" field
Designers manually copy completion dates into next steps text. This is error-prone and creates drift. Auto-populating would keep them in sync and reduce a repeated manual task.
