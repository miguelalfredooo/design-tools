Exploration D — shadcn + Vivid Accents

shadcn/ui zinc dark foundation. Three vivid accent colors — red, green, cyan — used only on the left border and type label. Everything else is neutral. High contrast where it matters, quiet everywhere else. Comfortable spacing with room to breathe. Geist font.

2 Risks
2 Opportunities
1 Pattern
Risk 3 of 5 participants
Users abandon the session setup flow when the objective field has no guidance or examples
Participants paused for 8–15 seconds on the objective field before either typing something generic or navigating away. Two participants explicitly said they didn't know what was expected. The empty state provides no scaffolding — no placeholder, no examples, no constraints on format.
Opportunity Cross-session synthesis
Confidence level in the summary strip could link directly to the supporting evidence
PMs consistently asked "why this confidence level?" after reading the summary. A direct link from the confidence indicator to the underlying data points would eliminate a follow-up question that currently requires the designer to explain verbally.
Pattern 4 of 5 participants
Participants consistently look for a "done" or "complete" action after reviewing results
After reviewing the results view, participants looked for a way to mark the session as complete or archive it. The current flow has no explicit completion action — sessions just become inactive. This creates ambiguity about whether a session is "done" or "paused."
Risk PM feedback — 2 incidents
The shared view exposes internal run IDs in the URL, confusing stakeholders
Two PMs separately asked what the alphanumeric string in the shared URL meant, assuming it was a version number or error code. Internal identifiers leaking into shared surfaces undermine the "readable labels" principle.
Opportunity Workflow observation
Design Ops module completion dates could auto-populate the "next steps" field
Designers manually copy completion dates into next steps text. This is error-prone and creates drift between the module status and the summary. Auto-populating would keep them in sync and reduce a repeated manual task.
