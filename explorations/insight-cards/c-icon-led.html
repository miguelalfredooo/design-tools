Exploration C — Icon-Led Monochrome

Type signalled by a shape icon in a muted container — not primarily by color. The icon encodes meaning (triangle for risk, arrow-up for opportunity, layers for pattern). Almost monochrome palette — color only whispers in the icon background. Typography does the heavy lifting. Shared view strips labels and compresses to essentials.

Risk
Users abandon the session setup flow when the objective field has no guidance or examples
3 of 5 participants — Session #12, #14, #15
Participants paused for 8–15 seconds on the objective field before either typing something generic or navigating away. Two participants explicitly said they didn't know what was expected. The empty state provides no scaffolding.
Opportunity
Confidence level in the summary strip could link directly to the supporting evidence
Cross-session synthesis
PMs consistently asked "why this confidence level?" after reading the summary. A direct link would eliminate this follow-up question entirely.
Pattern
Participants consistently look for a "done" or "complete" action after reviewing results
4 of 5 participants — Sessions #11–#15
After reviewing results, participants looked for a way to mark the session as complete. Sessions just become inactive, creating ambiguity about done vs. paused.
Risk
The shared view exposes internal run IDs in the URL, confusing stakeholders
PM feedback — 2 incidents
Two PMs separately asked what the alphanumeric string in the shared URL meant. Internal identifiers in shared surfaces undermine the "readable labels" principle.
Opportunity
Design Ops module completion dates could auto-populate the "next steps" field
Workflow observation
Designers manually copy completion dates into next steps. Auto-populating would keep them in sync and reduce a repeated manual task.
